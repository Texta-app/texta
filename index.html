FAQ

Common questions.

What is a text segment? +

A standard text message holds up to 160 characters. That's one segment — one cent. If your message is longer than 160 characters, it splits into multiple segments and costs one cent each. Most short messages are a single segment. The app shows you the segment count as you write so there are no surprises.

What about emojis and special characters? +

Certain characters — like curly quotes, em dashes, or some emojis — trigger a different encoding that reduces segment size to 70 characters. The app warns you the moment this happens and shows you the fix. For best results, keep messages in plain text.

Do I pay to receive replies? +

The first 500 inbound messages per month are on us. After that, replies cost the same as an outgoing text — one cent per segment. Most accounts never come close to 500 inbound messages in a month. If you run a campaign that asks people to text back, we show your current month inbound count in the dashboard so you always know where you stand.

How do replies work? +

Every inbound reply shows up in your Replies tab, matched to the contact name if we recognize the number. You get an email notification when someone responds so you never miss it. If a contact replies STOP (or UNSUBSCRIBE, QUIT, END, or CANCEL), they are automatically removed from future sends. You do not need to manage opt-outs manually.

What is the link shortener and do I need it? +

If you include a URL in your message, Texta automatically shortens it to a go.texta.me link before sending. You do not have to do anything — it just happens. This saves you characters (and therefore segments), which saves you money. It also lets you see exactly how many people clicked your link, right in your send history. Long URLs can eat up a significant chunk of your 160-character segment, so shortening them is an easy win on both cost and performance tracking.

What happens when my credit runs low? +

You can set up auto-recharge so your balance tops up automatically when it drops below a threshold you choose. Or add credits manually any time. Either way you stay in control and your campaigns never get cut off unexpectedly.

Can I send images? +

Yes. Image messages (MMS) cost 2.5 cents each, regardless of file size. You can attach photos, promotional graphics, or GIFs directly in the compose window. That's still cheaper than what the major competitors charge for plain text.

Can I cancel anytime? +

Yes. No contracts, no cancellation fees. Your unused message credits never expire — if you cancel and come back later, your credits are still there waiting for you.
